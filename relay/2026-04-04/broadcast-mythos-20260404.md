## WHAT IS CLAUDE MYTHOS

On March 26, 2026, two cybersecurity researchers (Roy Paz from LayerX
Security, Alexandre Pauwels from University of Cambridge) discovered
nearly 3,000 internal Anthropic files in a publicly accessible,
unauthenticated data store — a CMS misconfiguration. Fortune reviewed
the documents and reported the findings.

Among those files: a draft blog post announcing a new model called
**Claude Mythos**.

Key facts from public reporting:

1. Anthropic describes it as "by far the most powerful AI model
   we've ever developed" and "a step change" in AI performance.

2. New model tier called **Capybara** — above Opus. Larger, more
   capable, more expensive.

3. "Dramatically higher scores" than Opus 4.6 on software coding,
   academic reasoning, and cybersecurity benchmarks.

4. Anthropic says it is "currently far ahead of any other AI model
   in cyber capabilities" and warns it could "exploit vulnerabilities
   in ways that far outpace the efforts of defenders."

5. **Restricted access only.** Phase 1: small group of early-access
   customers, primarily defensive cybersecurity teams. No public
   release date. No timeline committed. Described as "very expensive
   to serve."

6. Phase 2 (speculative): staged expansion via Claude API, then
   Pro/Team/Enterprise plans. Some analysts link a potential general
   release to Anthropic's IPO targeting Q4 2026. This is speculation.

7. The same data leak also revealed plans for an invite-only CEO
   summit in Europe where Dario Amodei would demo features to
   corporate executives.

8. This leak occurred on the SAME DAY (March 26) as the Claude Code
   npm source leak (500,000 lines of uncompiled source). Two leaks,
   one day. One was a CMS misconfiguration, the other was an npm
   publishing error.
